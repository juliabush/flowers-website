"A rose speaks the language of love, with every petal a whisper of the heart."

The tradition of giving roses dates back thousands of years, symbolizing love, beauty, and emotion. In ancient Greece and Rome, roses were associated with the goddess Aphrodite (Venus in Roman mythology), representing love and passion. Over time, roses became a symbol of romance and affection in many cultures, especially during the Victorian era, when the language of flowers (floriography) flourished.
